About Operation: Adulting

The First Millennial & Gen-Z run hypeOrg

Operation: Adulting was formed to mentor and educate underprivileged minority and LGBTQIA+ high school and college seniors; we're bridging the gap of financial knowledge and the access to that knowledge in order to provide a more solid foundation for future innovators.

Operation: Adulting officially received its 501 (C) (3) in 2020 and is commited to making transformative change. Our board is made up of diverse millenials passionate about leavinng a better tomorrow for the generations after us.

What we offer

We assist under 21s with responsibly navigating their independence while guiding over 21s during their transition into adulthood.

O: A is here to equip under-represented & marginalised future generations with knowledge & tools in the areas of professional development, financial literacy, and personal growth.

Having transitioned into the digital age and everyone becomes accustomed to the new “normal” we need to remind our young adults of not only their IRL (In Real Life) reputation but the importance of their digital identities.

Embarking on the journey of their lifetime, our focus is ensuring they have all the tools they need to solidify their foundation and the knowledge of effective ways to use those tools.

FinLit101

Fiancial Literacy

Operation Adulting’s Financial Literacy program includes equiping our students with knowledge of Money Management: appropriate budgeting tips, saving for unexpected circumstances, repaying student loans, stock/investing unemployment benefits, and the EBT application process. Our high school students focus on: applying for scholarships, financial aid, and grants, explaining the FASFA process, and, most importantly, avoiding the credit card scam.

Our college-aged young adults concentrate on student loan repayment, investments such as the 401K, IRA, CDs, precious metals/ETFs Evergreen Investments, real estate/property, landlord versus management companies, tenants' and landlords' rights, and lottery apartments.

ID2022

Inner Engineering

Our Inner Engineering program equips our students with knowledge on Digital Identity management, explains the importance of understanding their e-rights, digital privacy rules and regulations; what to do/who to report "x" to; and the advantages and disadvantages of RESPONDing versus REACTing.

"Civil" Identity management covers the legal structure within society and the laws enforcing community and the advantages and disadvantages of RESPONDing versus REACTing.
Cultural Confidence introduces our young adults to worlds and cultures other than their own. What better way to learn new communication methods than to immerse ourselves in a new world?
Ethics and morals are related in that you base your decisions on your moral compass. We will discuss real-world scenarios that young people face and find resources to help you with your ethical quandaries.

JobZone +021

Professional development

Our "JobZone" includes information on writing a resume and cover letter, interviewing and networking tips/information, negotiating and hiring contracts, and following up after the interview.

021: Networking with our HHH (Home Happy Hour) fundraising event. There may be careers in Science, Space, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics that our young adults have never considered or heard of.

Pre-COVID, O:A would collaborate with companies and their organizations to host corporate sponsored shadow days for students to experience "A Day in the Life" of their future career title.

Not only does this give our young adults a glimpse into the future they strive for, but it is also a fantastic opportunity for businesses to see how future generations perceive approaching and tackle current and perhaps, future issues.

Web Development

Programming and Design

Our Web Development Project educates and facilitates our Interns to build complete projects that demonstrate their newly acquired skills in specific frontend web designs using design thinking and the conceptual frameworks of UX/UI (User Interface/User Experience) Design for creating their own personal or business websites, as well as web development programming languages such as HTML, CSS, Java/JavaScript, and Python.

Preparing these young adults to progress to a more advanced web development or design course or specialty
